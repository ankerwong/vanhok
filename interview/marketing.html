CityU BBA Marketing

90秒自介、群面讨论、营销策划与数字营销题库。

第三部分：CityU BBA Marketing——已知面试形式与考察逻辑

官方确立规则

  • 面试形式与安排： JUPAS 面试为 On a selective basis，全英文进行。无笔试环节（No written test is required）。
  • 核心流程： 包含 90-second self-introduction（90秒个人展示） + 15-minute group interview（15分钟无领导小组讨论）。
  • 群面高频任务： 围绕时事热点（current affairs）、社交议题讨论，或根据特定情境现场拟定营销计划（marketing plan on site）、情境问答（situational questions）。
  • 项目特征： 强调 i-Marketing+ 理念（interactive / international / intelligence），课程高度偏向数字营销与实务。设有两个分支：Global Marketing 与 Marketing Analytics。
  • 录取权重： 学术成绩（HKDSE等）仍是首要标准，但面试表现（Interview performance）将直接影响最终录取的优先级。

高概率考察逻辑： 城大营销的群面并不只看“谁英语流利”，而更看重商业敏感度、框架化表达、在群面中推进讨论的能力，以及能否将抽象的消费者洞察（Customer Insight）落地为可执行的商业策略（Actionable Strategies）。寻找的是具备极强数字网感（Digital Awareness）且懂得跨文化倾听的破冰者。

第八部分：BBA Marketing 高频问题库与参考答案（20题）

分组一：动机与项目匹配

Q1. Why marketing now?

考察点： 专业动机是否真实且符合时代。
回答结构： 先定义营销价值，再连接数字时代变化，最后落到个人兴趣。
I want to study marketing now because marketing is no longer just about promotion. It sits at the intersection of consumer psychology, data, creativity, and business strategy. In the digital era, brands have to understand not only what people buy, but why they trust, share, and stay loyal. That complexity attracts me. I enjoy observing how small changes in positioning, timing, or channel choice can completely change customer response. Marketing feels intellectually dynamic and commercially real at the same time, which is why it fits both my personality and my long-term career interest.
点评： 没把 marketing 说成“会卖东西”那么浅，而是点出了 psychology、data、strategy 的交叉属性。

Q2. Why CityU BBA Marketing?

考察点： 对项目特征是否了解。
回答结构： 项目定位 + 两个stream + 实务机会。
CityU BBA Marketing attracts me because it is clearly practical and digital-facing, rather than purely theoretical. I especially like the i-Marketing+ direction, because it combines interaction with industry, international exposure, and intelligence in the sense of digital and analytical thinking. The two streams also make sense to me. Global Marketing builds cross-cultural perspective, while Marketing Analytics strengthens data-driven decision-making. On top of that, the consultancy projects and internship exposure matter a lot to me, because I want to test ideas against real market responses, not just discuss them in class.
点评： 把项目卖点说得很完整：digital、international、analytics、consultancy，体现充分匹配度。

Q3. What is marketing beyond advertising?

考察点： 对 marketing 本质的理解。
回答结构： 先纠偏，再从 customer insight、value creation、positioning 展开。
To me, marketing is about understanding people well enough to create, communicate, and deliver value in a way that changes behavior. Advertising is only one visible output. Before that, there is segmentation, consumer insight, product-market fit, pricing, channel choice, and customer experience. If a product is badly positioned, no amount of advertising will save it for long. So good marketing is not just about making something look attractive. It is about aligning what a company offers with what a specific group of customers truly needs and is willing to choose.
点评： 回答有本质判断，能把营销从“传播”提升到“价值创造与行为改变”的层面。

Q4. Which stream suits you better, Global Marketing or Marketing Analytics?

考察点： 自我认知与未来方向。
回答结构： 明确选择 + 个人优势 + 保留另一方向的重要性。
At this stage, I am slightly more drawn to Marketing Analytics because I like making decisions based on evidence rather than instinct alone. I enjoy working with patterns, comparisons, and measurable outcomes, so I find the idea of turning customer data into strategic action very exciting. That said, I do not see analytics as separate from human insight. Numbers can tell us what is happening, but not always why. That is why I still value the global and cultural side of marketing. Ideally, I want to become someone who can read both dashboards and people.
点评： 选择明确，但没有贬低另一stream，表现出 balanced judgment，很像成熟申请者。

Q5. Give us your 90-second self-introduction.

考察点： 英文表达、信息筛选与个人定位。
回答结构： 身份标签 + 2个亮点经历 + why marketing。
Good afternoon. My name is Jason, and I am a student who is deeply interested in how ideas influence people’s decisions. Over the past few years, I found myself consistently drawn to activities that involved communication, teamwork, and strategy. For example, when organizing a school charity event, I enjoyed not only the event itself but also thinking about audience messaging, poster design, and how to increase participation. I also became more interested in digital consumer behavior through observing how short-form content changes attention and purchase intent. I want to study marketing because it combines creativity with analysis, and I believe CityU would be an ideal environment for me to develop both.
点评： 这类 90 秒版本信息密度够高，语气自然，适合作为群面前的 opening impression。

分组二：商业敏感度与营销判断

Q6. Tell us about a recent campaign you think worked well.

考察点： 对真实品牌动作的观察力。
回答结构： 先点品牌，再分析受众、信息、渠道、结果。
One type of campaign I find effective recently is when a brand combines short-form video with a clear community angle instead of just pushing product features. What works is not simply high exposure, but relevance. The best campaigns understand the audience’s language, platform behavior, and emotional trigger. For example, if a brand targets younger consumers, it cannot sound like a formal corporate announcement. It needs native content, relatable creators, and a reason for people to comment or share. To me, the strongest campaigns succeed because they match message, platform, and audience behavior instead of treating visibility as the only goal.
点评： 即使不死背具体案例，也展现了对“受众—平台—内容匹配”的真实营销判断。

Q7. Tell us about a campaign or brand move you think failed.

考察点： 批判性判断与边界意识。
回答结构： 先指出问题，再分析错位点，最后给更好做法。
I think some brand moves fail when they chase trends too obviously without understanding the brand’s own voice. For example, when a traditional brand suddenly tries to sound overly youthful on social media, the content can feel forced rather than fresh. Consumers are very sensitive to inauthenticity, especially online. The problem is usually not using a trend itself, but using it without strategic fit. A better approach would be to keep the brand’s core identity intact while adapting tone and format gradually. Relevance matters, but coherence matters just as much.
点评： 不是简单说“这个广告不好”，而是点出了 brand voice 与 trend chasing 的错位。

Q8. How would you market a new sugar-free drink to university students in Hong Kong?

考察点： 基本营销框架与实操能力。
回答结构： Target audience + positioning + channel + activation + KPI。
I would start by defining a narrow target instead of saying “all students.” For example, I might focus on health-conscious but busy students who want a drink that feels light, modern, and convenient. The positioning should go beyond “sugar-free” because that alone can sound functional. I would frame it around clean energy, lifestyle fit, and better everyday choices. In terms of channels, I would combine campus sampling, short video content, student creators, and location-based promotions near universities. For KPIs, I would track trial, repeat purchase, and content engagement separately, because buzz without repurchase is not enough.
点评： 标准高分框架：目标人群、定位、渠道、KPI 一条线走通，没有只停留在“做小红书/IG”。

Q9. If your campaign budget were cut by 50%, what would you do?

考察点： 资源受限下的优先级判断。
回答结构： 保核心目标 + 收缩渠道 + 强化高ROI动作。
My first step would be to revisit the campaign objective. If the objective is trial, awareness tactics and conversion tactics should not be treated equally. With half the budget, I would cut low-efficiency channels first and protect the activities closest to the objective. I would also narrow the audience instead of trying to reach everyone weakly. In many cases, a smaller but more precisely targeted campaign performs better than a broad but diluted one. Budget pressure forces discipline, and good marketers should know what to keep, what to simplify, and what to stop.
点评： 体现出 ROI 和 prioritization 思维，符合商学院面试对“商业脑”的期待。

Q10. What matters more: creativity or conversion?

考察点： 是否会平衡品牌与效果。
回答结构： 不做二选一 + 区分阶段 + 给出自己的立场。
I would not treat creativity and conversion as enemies. Creativity earns attention and memorability, while conversion proves business impact. The real question is what stage the brand is in and what the objective is. If nobody notices your message, conversion tools have limited room to work. But if a campaign is highly creative and generates discussion without influencing behavior, it becomes performative. My view is that creativity should serve strategic clarity. The best campaigns are creatively distinctive and commercially accountable at the same time.
点评： 很成熟的回答：不被带进假二选一，而是用 objective 来化解对立。

分组三：数字营销、数据与 AI

Q11. Do brands need to take positions on social issues?

考察点： 品牌立场、风险判断与价值观。
回答结构： 承认机会 + 指出风险 + 强调一致性。
Brands can take positions on social issues, but they should do so carefully and only when there is real alignment with their values, operations, and audience expectations. If a brand speaks out only when it is fashionable, it risks looking opportunistic. On the other hand, complete silence is not always neutral either. I think the key is consistency. If a company claims to care about sustainability, for example, its supply chain and product choices should reflect that. Otherwise, the message becomes a reputational risk rather than a strength.
点评： 展现了很好的 boundary awareness：不是反对品牌发声，而是强调 authentic alignment。

Q12. Is influencer marketing still trustworthy?

考察点： 对 creator economy 的现实判断。
回答结构： 先给结论，再拆分“可信”的条件。
It is still valuable, but trust is no longer automatic. Audiences are much more aware of sponsored content now, so relevance and authenticity matter more than follower count. A smaller creator with a credible relationship with a niche community can be more effective than a celebrity with broad but shallow reach. I also think brands should evaluate more than impressions. They need to look at comment quality, audience fit, and whether the creator’s style actually matches the brand. Influencer marketing still works, but lazy influencer marketing works far less than before.
点评： 不是简单说 KOL 过时，而是指出信任机制变了，思考很贴近当下市场。

Q13. How is AI changing marketing, and where should marketers be careful?

考察点： AI 机会与风险的平衡理解。
回答结构： 效率提升 + 创意辅助 + 风险边界。
AI is making marketing faster in areas like content ideation, customer segmentation, ad optimization, and trend analysis. It reduces repetitive work and helps teams test more variations quickly. However, marketers should be careful not to confuse speed with judgment. AI can generate polished outputs, but it does not automatically understand cultural nuance, brand tone, or reputational risk. There are also concerns around originality, bias, and over-personalization. I think AI should be treated as an accelerator, not a substitute for strategic thinking and human accountability.
点评： 把 AI 定位为 accelerator 而不是替代者，判断稳健，很符合面试高分答案的气质。

Q14. How should companies use customer data without crossing ethical lines?

考察点： 数据伦理与商业边界。
回答结构： 合法合规 + 透明 + 最小必要 + 用户价值。
Companies should use customer data in a way that is transparent, proportionate, and genuinely useful to the customer. Consent should not be hidden inside vague terms, and data collection should follow a minimum-necessary principle rather than a “collect everything first” mindset. Personalization can improve user experience, but when it becomes intrusive, it damages trust. I think the key test is simple: if customers clearly understood how their data was being used, would they still feel respected? Ethical marketing is not anti-data. It is about using data in a way that preserves trust over the long term.
点评： 不是停留在 privacy 口号，而是给出了 transparent、minimum-necessary 这种成熟原则。

Q15. How would you market to Gen Z without sounding fake?

考察点： 对年轻消费者的理解。
回答结构： 先指出常见误区，再给真实策略。
I would avoid treating Gen Z as a stereotype defined only by slang and trends. What usually sounds fake is when a brand tries to imitate youth culture without understanding its values or context. I would focus on clarity, relevance, and respect. That means using the right platform behavior, speaking in a natural tone, and offering something that genuinely fits their lives, whether that is convenience, identity, community, or value for money. I would also listen closely to user feedback because Gen Z consumers tend to respond quickly when a brand feels performative.
点评： 抓住了关键：不是学年轻人说话，而是真正理解他们重视什么。

分组四：群面协作、表达与领导力

Q16. What is the difference between a viral campaign and an effective campaign?

考察点： 对传播与商业结果的区分能力。
回答结构： 先定义差异，再说明两者重合但不等同。
A viral campaign is one that spreads quickly. An effective campaign is one that achieves its objective. Sometimes the two overlap, but not always. A campaign can generate millions of views and still fail to improve brand perception, trial, or sales. On the other hand, a more targeted campaign with modest reach can be highly effective if it changes the behavior of the right audience. That is why marketers need to define success before launching the campaign. Virality is a distribution outcome. Effectiveness is a business outcome.
点评： 一句“distribution outcome vs business outcome”很有商科面试辨识度。

Q17. How would you respond to a wave of negative comments online?

考察点： 危机沟通与品牌信任管理。
回答结构： 先判断性质，再分层回应，最后修复信任。
I would first distinguish between three situations: misunderstanding, service failure, and value-based backlash. They require different responses. If there is a factual misunderstanding, the brand should clarify quickly and calmly. If customers are complaining because the brand really failed, then the response should include ownership, apology, and a visible fix rather than polished wording alone. If it is a deeper reputational issue, then the company may need a broader review of policy or messaging. The key is not to become defensive too early. In a crisis, tone matters as much as speed.
点评： 把负面评论拆成三类，展现出非常好的 crisis triage 思维。

Q18. How are Hong Kong and Mainland Chinese consumers different in some categories?

考察点： 跨市场认知与细分意识。
回答结构： 先强调不能过度概括，再谈平台、信任、决策路径差异。
I would be careful not to overgeneralize, because both markets are internally diverse. That said, in some categories there can be meaningful differences in platform behavior, payment habits, and trust formation. Mainland consumers may be more deeply integrated into all-in-one digital ecosystems and livestream commerce, while Hong Kong consumers may respond differently depending on category, especially when offline convenience, international brand image, or local credibility matters. So I would not start with assumptions. I would start with category-specific research and platform-specific behavior.
点评： 很稳。先反对 stereotype，再给出平台生态与决策路径差异，跨境视野到位。

Q19. Tell us about a time you influenced a team without formal authority.

考察点： 说服力与协作推进能力。
回答结构： 冲突点 + 你的介入方式 + 结果。
In one group project, our team kept discussing ideas but was making no actual progress because everyone preferred different directions. I was not the leader, but I suggested that we stop debating abstract preferences and list three criteria: feasibility, audience fit, and time required. Once we evaluated each idea using the same criteria, the discussion became much calmer. My preferred idea was not chosen in full, but the team moved forward with a hybrid version that everyone accepted. That experience taught me that influence often comes from structuring the conversation, not dominating it.
点评： 非常适合群面项目：不是抢领导，而是通过“给框架”来获得影响力。

Q20. In a group discussion, what would you do if one person spoke too much and another stayed silent?

考察点： 群面控场与团队气氛管理。
回答结构： 先稳住氛围，再温和限流，最后主动邀请他人加入。
I would try to manage it without making the atmosphere awkward. If one person is speaking too much, I would not shut them down directly. Instead, I might say, “That’s a useful point. Maybe we can also hear from others before we decide.” Then I would invite the quieter member in a low-pressure way, such as asking whether they agree with the current direction or if they see another angle. In group discussions, I think good performance is not about speaking the most. It is about helping the group think more clearly and work more effectively.
点评： 这就是群面高分动作：温和限流、主动拉人、以 group outcome 为导向。
