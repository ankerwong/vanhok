CUHK MBChB 临床医学

个人面试、医学动机、伦理与公共卫生题库。

第一部分：CUHK MBChB 临床医学——已知面试形式与考察逻辑

官方确立规则

  • 面试形式： 个人面对两位考官的座谈面试（Individual interview by two panel members）。
  • 语言与时长： 全英文进行，时长约 15 分钟。
  • 考察核心点： Interest in medicine, general knowledge about health care, opinions about ethics and humanism, teamwork, leadership, communication。
  • 面试次数： 仅面试一次，无多轮筛选。
  • 着装要求： 必须穿着正式（Dress formally）。

高概率考察逻辑： 面试呈半结构化，考官通常持有申请者的个人陈述（PS）与简历。极大可能从 PS 中的某个细节切入，追问课外活动反思，并迅速过渡到香港医疗现状或情境伦理题。

第六部分：MBChB 高频问题库与参考答案（20题）

分组一：动机与职业理解

Q1. Why medicine, now?

考察点： 学医的根本驱动力与时效性。
回答结构： 科学兴趣 + 人文触动 + 长期承诺的认知。
My desire to study medicine stems from a blend of scientific curiosity and a profound need to make a tangible impact on individuals' lives. Growing up, I was fascinated by human biology, but the turning point was volunteering at a local clinic. I witnessed how a physician not only treated physical ailments but also provided an emotional anchor during patients' most vulnerable moments. I realized that medicine uniquely operates at the intersection of science and humanity. While I understand the immense pressure and lifelong commitment required, the privilege of translating scientific knowledge into concrete care is a responsibility I am eager to undertake today.
点评： 开门见山，点出 science + humanity，并主动提及自己对高压和终身承诺的心理准备，显得成熟。

Q2. Why CUHK Medicine?

考察点： 对中大医学院特色的调研与匹配度。
回答结构： 早期临床接触 + 科研导向/学院氛围 + 个人契合度。
I chose CUHK because of its distinct emphasis on early clinical exposure and its strong track record in pioneering research, particularly in gastroenterology and oncology. The supportive, tight-knit community here resonates with my collaborative learning style. Furthermore, CUHK’s bioethics curriculum stands out to me. I believe being a good doctor requires more than just clinical excellence; it requires a strong moral compass. CUHK provides the perfect ecosystem where I can develop both my scientific acumen and my humanistic values from day one.
点评： 提到了早期临床接触、特色科研以及生物伦理课程，证明申请者做过深度调研，而非泛泛而谈。

Q3. What makes a good doctor in today’s healthcare system?

考察点： 职业价值观与现代医疗环境认知。
回答结构： 扎实的临床能力 + 同理心沟通 + 应对系统压力的韧性。
A good doctor today needs a balance of clinical excellence, profound empathy, and systemic adaptability. Competence is non-negotiable—a doctor must stay updated with rapid medical advancements. However, what truly elevates a doctor is the ability to communicate with compassion, recognizing the patient as a whole person, not just a set of symptoms. Additionally, in today's strained healthcare systems, a good doctor must be a team player and possess the resilience to deliver quality care despite resource limitations and administrative pressures.
点评： 超越了传统的“医术+医德”，加入了“系统适应力/韧性”，非常契合当前的真实医疗环境。

Q4. Tell us about a moment that confirmed medicine was right for you.

考察点： 经历的真实性与个人反思深度。
回答结构： 情境描述 + 观察到的关键细节 + 内心确认。
During my shadowing experience in an oncology ward, I observed a consultation where a doctor had to deliver a difficult prognosis to a patient. Instead of rushing through the medical jargon, the doctor sat down at eye level, held the patient's hand, and patiently explained the options while acknowledging their fear. It wasn't a moment of miraculous cure, but a moment of profound human connection. Seeing how a doctor could alleviate suffering even when curing wasn't possible confirmed for me that this is the exact kind of meaningful work I want to dedicate my life to.
点评： 选取了“无法治愈但能安慰”的真实临床痛点，展现了申请者对医学局限性的成熟认知。

Q5. If you are not admitted this year, what would you do?

考察点： 抗挫折能力与对医学的执着度。
回答结构： 坦承失落 + 寻求反馈 + 具体提升计划 + 再次申请。
If I am not admitted this year, I will naturally be disappointed, but it won't deter my long-term goal. First, I would critically evaluate my application and seek feedback to identify my blind spots. In the meantime, I would pursue a related degree, such as biomedical sciences, to build a robust academic foundation. I would also engage in more healthcare volunteering, perhaps focusing on elderly care or palliative care, to deepen my clinical understanding and empathy. Ultimately, I would definitely reapply, as medicine remains my true vocation.
点评： 态度不卑不亢，行动计划具体（如去学相关专业、做特定领域的义工），展现极强的心理韧性。

分组二：香港医疗系统与公共卫生

Q6. What do you think is the biggest pressure facing Hong Kong’s public healthcare system?

考察点： 公共卫生常识与系统性思维。
回答结构： 点出核心问题（公私营失衡/人手短缺）+ 连带后果 + 解决思路。
The most prominent pressure is the severe imbalance between the public and private sectors, exacerbated by chronic manpower shortages. The public sector cares for roughly 90% of inpatients while employing less than half of the city's doctors. This leads to burnout among healthcare professionals and unacceptably long waiting times for patients, especially in specialist outpatient clinics. While the Voluntary Health Insurance Scheme (VHIS) attempts to divert some burden to the private sector, I believe a long-term solution requires aggressively expanding primary care networks to keep patients out of hospitals in the first place.
点评： 数据准确（90%住院病人），直接切中公立医院人手流失的痛点，并提出了基层医疗的解决方向。

Q7. How should Hong Kong respond to ageing and chronic disease?

考察点： 对老龄化社会的应对策略。
回答结构： 现状挑战 + 预防胜于治疗 + 社区医疗赋能。
With a rapidly ageing population, chronic diseases like diabetes and hypertension are overwhelming our acute hospital beds. Hong Kong must shift its paradigm from a hospital-centric model to a community-based, preventive model. The development of District Health Centres (DHCs) is a step in the right direction. By empowering allied health professionals—like dietitians and physiotherapists—to manage chronic conditions at the neighborhood level, we can reduce acute exacerbations. Furthermore, leveraging telehealth for routine monitoring of elderly patients can ensure continuous care without adding to hospital congestion.
点评： 提到了具体的政策（DHCs）和多学科团队（Allied health）概念，展现了现代医疗大局观。

Q8. Is strengthening primary care more important than expanding hospitals?

考察点： 资源分配的权衡能力。
回答结构： 辩证看待两者作用 + 明确倾向基层医疗的长远价值。
Both are necessary, but strengthening primary care is the more sustainable strategy in the long run. Expanding hospitals is a reactive measure; it addresses the symptoms of a strained system but doesn't solve the root cause. Primary care, on the other hand, acts as the gatekeeper. By focusing on health promotion, early screening, and chronic disease management in the community, we can significantly reduce the number of patients requiring expensive and resource-intensive acute hospital care. It’s about shifting the focus from treating illness to maintaining wellness.
点评： 逻辑清晰的对比论证（Reactive vs. Preventive），用“Gatekeeper”一词精准概括了基层医疗的作用。

Q9. Why is antimicrobial resistance (AMR) a serious issue even if patients rarely notice it?

考察点： 对全球性公共卫生隐患的理解。
回答结构： 解释危害性质 + 临床后果 + 医生的守门人责任。
AMR is often called a "silent pandemic." It is critically serious because it undermines the foundation of modern medicine. If common antibiotics lose their efficacy, routine procedures like cesarean sections, joint replacements, and chemotherapy become high-risk endeavors due to untreatable infections. Patients may not notice it until they are directly affected, but as future doctors, we must act as stewards of antibiotics. This means resisting the pressure to overprescribe for viral infections and educating the public on medication adherence to preserve these life-saving drugs for the future.
点评： 用“Silent pandemic”破题，点出抗生素失效对现代医学手术的毁灭性打击，体现了医学专业敏感度。

Q10. What are the benefits and limitations of telemedicine?

考察点： 科技在医疗中应用的边界感。
回答结构： 优势（可及性/效率）+ 局限（体格检查/数字鸿沟/隐私）+ 总结。
Telemedicine significantly enhances accessibility, allowing patients with mobility issues or minor ailments to consult doctors without traveling, thereby reducing waiting room congestion. However, its limitations are substantial. A screen cannot replace a comprehensive physical examination, which is often crucial for an accurate diagnosis. Additionally, there is a risk of widening the "digital divide," where elderly or low-income patients who lack technological literacy are left behind. Therefore, telemedicine should be viewed as an excellent triage and follow-up tool, rather than a total replacement for in-person clinical care.
点评： 辩证分析非常到位，尤其指出了“数字鸿沟（Digital divide）”这一常被忽视的社会公平议题。

分组三：医学伦理与沟通

Q11. What are the opportunities and risks of AI in healthcare?

考察点： 前沿科技的伦理双刃剑。
回答结构： 机会（效率/筛查）+ 风险（算法偏见/责任归属/医患关系）。
AI offers tremendous opportunities in healthcare, particularly in pattern recognition, such as screening radiological images faster and sometimes more accurately than the human eye. This can alleviate doctor burnout. However, the risks are heavily rooted in ethics and accountability. If an AI makes a misdiagnosis, who is legally responsible? Furthermore, AI algorithms can inherit biases from their training data, potentially leading to unequal care for minority groups. Ultimately, AI should remain an augmenting tool, a "co-pilot," ensuring that the final clinical judgment and empathy remain distinctly human.
点评： 准确指出了“算法偏见”和“责任归属”两大核心风险，定位 AI 为“Co-pilot”十分稳妥。

Q12. Would you trust AI to support diagnosis?

考察点： 对辅助工具的批判性接受能力。
回答结构： 有条件信任 + 解释条件（透明度/人类监督）。
I would trust AI to support a diagnosis, but only with cautious oversight. I view AI as a highly capable second opinion. It can flag anomalies that I might miss during a long shift. However, trust in AI requires transparency—we need to understand how the algorithm reaches its conclusions. More importantly, an AI doesn't understand the patient's socioeconomic background, their anxieties, or their unique context. Therefore, while I would leverage AI's analytical power, the final diagnostic decision must always integrate the AI's data with my holistic clinical assessment.
点评： 强调了机器不懂患者的社会经济背景和情绪，突出了医生不可替代的综合判断力。

Q13. Should families be able to override a patient’s wishes in end-of-life care?

考察点： 患者自主权（Autonomy）与家属意愿的冲突。
回答结构： 确认核心原则（自主权）+ 理解家属动机 + 沟通策略。
Ethically and legally, a competent patient's advance directive or expressed wishes should always take precedence, rooted in the principle of autonomy. However, in reality, facing grieving families requires immense sensitivity. Families usually want to override wishes out of love and an inability to let go. Instead of simply citing the law, my role is to facilitate empathetic communication. I would sit with the family, validate their grief, and gently remind them of what the patient valued most in their life. The goal is to guide the family to accept the patient's wishes peacefully.
点评： 完美展示了伦理原则。先讲原则（自主权第一），再谈实操（共情家属、引导接受），极具临床智慧。

Q14. How would you discuss organ donation with a hesitant family?

考察点： 敏感话题的沟通技巧与同理心。
回答结构： 创造安全环境 + 倾听顾虑 + 提供信息而不强迫。
Discussing organ donation requires timing, extreme empathy, and respect. I would never rush the conversation. First, I would ensure they have fully understood and accepted the prognosis. Then, I would introduce the topic gently, creating a pressure-free space. If they are hesitant, my priority is to actively listen to their concerns—whether they stem from religious beliefs or a desire to keep the body intact. I would provide clear, factual information about how the process respects the deceased, but I would never coerce them. The family's emotional well-being and closure always come first.
点评： 强调了“倾听顾虑”和“绝不强迫（never coerce）”，这正是医学伦理中“无害（Non-maleficence）”的体现。

Q15. What concerns you about adolescent mental health today?

考察点： 心理健康视野与社会决定因素。
回答结构： 现状观察 + 社交媒体的双刃剑 + 医疗干预局限。
I am deeply concerned by the rising rates of anxiety and depression among adolescents, heavily fueled by social media. Platforms often create an illusion of perfection, leading to severe body image issues and cyberbullying. It disrupts their sleep architecture and replaces physical social interactions. While social media can offer support communities for marginalized youths, the algorithm-driven comparison culture is toxic. As a future healthcare provider, I realize that treating these issues requires more than prescriptions; it requires holistic counseling, educating parents, and advocating for healthier digital habits in schools.
点评： 准确抓住了社交媒体导致的身材焦虑、睡眠剥夺问题，并提出了超越开药的整体干预思路。

分组四：团队、领导与韧性

Q16. Tell us about a time you worked with someone difficult.

考察点： 冲突解决能力与情商。
回答结构： STAR模型 + 聚焦沟通与共同目标。
During a group research project, one team member consistently missed deadlines and seemed disengaged, causing frustration among the rest of us. Instead of confronting him aggressively, I asked him for a private chat over coffee. I discovered he was struggling with personal family issues and felt overwhelmed. We mutually agreed to reallocate some of his data-entry tasks to others, while he took on the final proofreading, which required less synchronous work. By approaching the situation with empathy rather than blame, we maintained team morale and delivered a high-quality project.
点评： 展现了“非暴力沟通”和换位思考的能力，处理方法务实（重新分配任务），结果双赢。

Q17. Tell us about a setback that changed how you work.

考察点： 学习能力与成长型思维。
回答结构： 描述挫折 + 错误反思 + 行为改变。
In my first year of debate club, I completely froze during a major regional tournament because the opposition brought up an argument I hadn't prepared for. We lost the round. I felt terrible, but it forced me to change my approach. I realized I was over-relying on memorized scripts instead of understanding the core concepts. From then on, I started practicing "stress-testing" my arguments with teammates, focusing on adaptability rather than rote memorization. This shift from rigid preparation to agile thinking has fundamentally improved how I handle unexpected academic and personal challenges.
点评： 挫折很真实，从“死记硬背”到“敏捷思考”的转变，恰好是学医非常需要的特质。

Q18. How do you respond when you do not know the answer?

考察点： 诚实度与求知欲。
回答结构： 坦诚不知 + 展现逻辑推导 + 表达后续查证意愿。
I believe intellectual honesty is crucial, especially in medicine where guessing can cause harm. If I am asked a question I don't know the answer to, I will admit it directly. However, I won't just stop there. I would try to walk through my thought process based on the knowledge I do possess. I might say, "I am not entirely sure about the specific mechanism, but given my understanding of related physiological principles, I would hypothesize that..." Finally, I would make it a point to research the exact answer afterward and follow up.
点评： 完美的临场反应框架。医学决不能不懂装懂，展现思考过程比给出正确答案更重要。

Q19. Tell us about a time you took the initiative to lead a team through a crisis.

考察点： 危机领导力与决断力。
回答结构： 突发危机 + 稳定军心并分配任务 + 最终结果。
During a school charity bake sale, our primary supplier canceled our massive pastry order just hours before the event. Panic set in among the volunteers. Recognizing the need for immediate action, I gathered the team and shifted our strategy. I delegated two members to buy raw ingredients from local supermarkets, while the rest of us turned the school's home economics room into an assembly line. I focused on keeping the atmosphere calm and encouraging. We ended up baking everything ourselves, and the "handmade" marketing angle actually helped us exceed our fundraising target.
点评： 例子生活化但极具说服力，展现了危机中的任务拆解和情绪稳定能力。

Q20. How do you plan to manage the intense stress of medical school and a clinical career?

考察点： 压力管理机制。
回答结构： 承认压力常态 + 具体的微观/宏观减压策略。
I see stress management not as a luxury, but as a professional responsibility to ensure I don't burn out and harm patients. On a micro level, I practice time-blocking to prevent academic overwhelm, and I use short mindfulness breathing exercises to quickly reset during stressful moments. On a macro level, I fiercely protect my time for physical exercise, particularly distance running, which gives me mental clarity. I also believe in the power of a strong support system—talking openly with peers who understand the struggle is vital for maintaining long-term emotional resilience.
点评： 将减压上升为“职业责任（professional responsibility）”，境界很高，策略具体可行。
