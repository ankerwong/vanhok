双学位定位、证据与科学、临床人文与系统判断题库。

第五部分：HKBU 中医学学士及生物医学理学士（荣誉）——已知面试形式与考察逻辑

官方确立规则

  • 课程性质： JS2410 为 6 年全日制双学位课程，含 15 个月 internship training 与 Honours Project。
  • 课程结构： 约 70% 聚焦 Chinese medicine，约 30% 聚焦 biomedical science 与 clinical western medicine。
  • 面试筛选： JUPAS 申请者如将课程列为 Band A 且学业表现良好，可获 shortlist 参加面试。
  • 面试轮次： 第一轮通常在 6 月中旬，第二轮在 HKDSE 放榜后；两轮 format 与 bonus 相同，shortlisted applicants 只会面试一次。
  • 语言： 面试主要以 English 与 Putonghua 进行；课程教学则会涉及 English / Putonghua / Cantonese。
  • 临床实践： 在学期间需到浸大中医诊所 practicum，五、六年级包含 12 个月内地中医教学医院实习与 3 个月专科临床实习。

高概率考察逻辑： 这类面试不会只看你是否“喜欢中医”，而是看你能否真正理解这是一门中医理论、现代生命科学、临床沟通与长期实践训练并重的双学位。高频追问通常集中在：为什么不是纯中医或纯 biomedical science、如何看待中医与现代证据体系的关系、怎样理解安全性与患者沟通、是否接受高强度临床训练，以及你能否在 English / Putonghua 语境下讲清楚复杂医疗概念。

第十部分：HKBU 中医学学士及生物医学理学士（荣誉） 高频问题库与参考答案（20题）

分组一：动机与双学位理解

Q1. Why this double degree instead of pure Chinese Medicine or pure Biomedical Science?

考察点： 对双学位定位的理解。
回答结构： 中医的人体整体观 + 生物医学的证据与机制 + 你想做桥梁。
I am drawn to this double degree because I do not want to choose too early between traditional clinical wisdom and modern biomedical explanation. Chinese Medicine offers a holistic way of seeing the patient, while Biomedical Science gives me a stronger language for mechanisms, safety, and evidence. I want to become someone who can think across both systems responsibly, rather than treating them as competing camps. That bridge is exactly what attracts me to this programme.
点评： 高分点在于“bridge”意识，而不是把两套体系简单拼盘化。

Q2. Why HKBU for this programme?

考察点： 对课程结构与实践要求的理解。
回答结构： 6年双学位 + clinic / internship + whole person education。
HKBU attracts me because this programme is not just academically broad but clinically serious. It combines Chinese Medicine, Biomedical Science, and clinical western medicine in a structured six-year pathway, and the practicum plus long internship means students are expected to grow into real practitioners, not just exam-takers. I also value the whole person education emphasis, because healthcare is not only about knowledge. It is also about judgment, communication, and integrity.
点评： 既点出 curriculum，也点出职业成熟度，非常贴合面试官期待。

Q3. What attracts you to Chinese Medicine in today’s world?

考察点： 中医动机是否真实且当代化。
回答结构： 患者整体性 + chronic care + 当代 relevance。
What attracts me most is that Chinese Medicine pays close attention to patterns, constitution, and the whole lived experience of the patient. In a time when many people are dealing with chronic conditions, stress, sleep issues, and long-term recovery, that whole-person perspective feels especially relevant. I do not see Chinese Medicine as a romantic alternative to everything else. I see it as a disciplined clinical tradition that still has meaningful value when practiced responsibly and thoughtfully.
点评： 没有神化中医，而是强调 responsible practice，分寸感很好。

Q4. How do you understand the relationship between Chinese Medicine and Biomedical Science?

考察点： 能否避免二元对立。
回答结构： 不同语言体系 + 可互补 + 需严谨转换。
I see them as different knowledge languages trying to understand health, disease, and recovery from different angles. Biomedical Science is strong in mechanisms, measurable markers, and controlled evidence, while Chinese Medicine is strong in pattern recognition, individualized judgment, and long-term regulation. I do not think either should be used carelessly to replace the other. The real challenge is learning when they complement each other and how to translate responsibly between them.
点评： 最忌讳“谁更高级”的对立表达，这个答案处理得很成熟。

Q5. This is a six-year programme with demanding clinical training. Why are you ready for it?

考察点： 长期承诺与抗压认知。
回答结构： 知道强度 + 接受训练周期 + 讲内在动力。
I am ready not because I think it will be easy, but because I understand why it must be demanding. A programme that combines two academic systems and clinical practice should be rigorous. It requires patience, discipline, and humility. I am prepared for the long training cycle because I do not want a superficial understanding of healthcare. If I choose this path, I want to earn the competence and trust that patients would eventually place in me.
点评： 强调“我知道它难，也接受它必须难”，比空喊 passion 更有说服力。

分组二：证据、科学与安全

Q6. Can Chinese Medicine be evidence-based?

考察点： 证据意识。
回答结构： 可以，但方法要匹配 + 不能口号化。
Yes, I believe Chinese Medicine can and should engage with evidence, but we need to be intellectually honest about how that evidence is built. Some aspects can be studied through trials, biomarkers, and outcome data, while other aspects may require more careful methodological design because individualized treatment is part of the tradition. To me, evidence-based does not mean forcing everything into one rigid template. It means being willing to test claims, examine safety, and improve practice through data rather than assumption alone.
点评： 既支持证据化，也承认方法学挑战，避免极端化。

Q7. How would you respond to someone who says Chinese Medicine is unscientific?

考察点： 沟通与理性辩护能力。
回答结构： 先理解质疑 + 区分历史传统与现代研究 + 回到临床责任。
I would not respond defensively. I would first acknowledge that some people are skeptical because they care about safety, mechanism, and reliable evidence, which is reasonable. Then I would explain that Chinese Medicine is a long clinical tradition, but modern healthcare still requires ongoing research, standardization, and careful practice. My goal would not be to win an argument. It would be to show that serious practitioners should welcome scrutiny, because patient welfare matters more than ideology.
点评： 先承认对方 concern，再回到 patient welfare，非常高分。

Q8. Why do future Chinese Medicine practitioners need biomedical knowledge?

考察点： 对双学位必要性的理解。
回答结构： 安全、沟通、协作、现代临床环境。
Biomedical knowledge matters because modern practitioners do not work in isolation. They need to understand red flags, communicate with other healthcare professionals, interpret tests more responsibly, and recognize when a patient needs urgent referral or co-management. It also strengthens patient safety, especially when dealing with complex conditions or concurrent treatments. To me, biomedical science does not weaken Chinese Medicine. It makes practice more responsible in a modern healthcare system.
点评： 用 safety 和 referral 说明必要性，避免空泛地说“更全面”。

Q9. How should safety be managed in herbal medicine?

考察点： 安全边界与临床责任。
回答结构： 质量控制 + 适应症判断 + 交互风险 + follow-up。
Safety in herbal medicine starts long before prescribing. It involves sourcing quality-controlled materials, understanding dosage and contraindications, checking for possible interactions with western medication, and following up closely on patient response. It also requires knowing when not to prescribe. I think one of the most dangerous attitudes in healthcare is overconfidence. A safe practitioner is not the one who uses the most tools, but the one who understands the limits and responsibilities of each tool.
点评： “knowing when not to prescribe” 是非常关键的高分信号。

Q10. What role can modern labs and biomarkers play in this field?

考察点： 现代科研视野。
回答结构： 支持理解与评估 + 不能替代全部临床判断。
Modern labs and biomarkers can help us evaluate safety, monitor response, and study mechanisms more clearly. They are especially useful in research and in integrated clinical settings where practitioners need to communicate across disciplines. At the same time, I would not reduce all healing to a lab value. Numbers are powerful, but patients are more than numbers. The strength of this double degree is that it trains students to respect both measurable evidence and clinical judgment.
点评： 处理好了“科学测量”与“整体病人”之间的张力。

分组三：临床、人文与系统判断

Q11. How would you explain a Chinese Medicine concept to a skeptical patient?

考察点： patient communication。
回答结构： 少术语 + 先讲目标 + 再讲 rationale。
I would avoid starting with technical language that sounds distant or mystical. I would begin by explaining the treatment goal in terms the patient already understands, such as improving sleep, reducing pain, or supporting recovery. Then I would explain the treatment rationale in a calm, transparent way and invite questions. If the patient remains unconvinced, I would respect that. Good communication is not about forcing belief. It is about helping patients make informed decisions with clarity and trust.
点评： 沟通重在 clarity and trust，不是证明自己“赢了”。

Q12. What makes a good Chinese Medicine practitioner today?

考察点： 职业理解。
回答结构： clinical skill + empathy + safety + openness to evidence。
A good practitioner today needs more than technical knowledge. They need sound clinical judgment, empathy, and the ability to listen carefully to what patients are really experiencing. They also need a strong sense of safety and professional humility. In today’s world, I think a good practitioner should be open to evidence, willing to communicate across disciplines, and honest about what their treatment can and cannot do. Trust is built through competence and integrity together.
点评： 很完整地覆盖了 competence、humility、integrity 三条主线。

Q13. What would you do if a patient insists on herbs but clearly needs urgent hospital care?

考察点： red flags 与转介意识。
回答结构： 先安全 + 明确转介 + 温和解释。
Patient safety must come first. If I believe the patient shows signs that require urgent hospital care, I would explain the risk clearly and recommend immediate referral rather than trying to manage the case within my own comfort zone. I would speak respectfully, because fear often drives resistance. But respect should not become passivity. A responsible practitioner must know when support means referring, not treating.
点评： 这是面试里非常关键的边界题，答案必须先把安全摆在第一位。

Q14. How should Chinese Medicine respond to mental health concerns among young people?

考察点： 当代社会议题与医疗敏感度。
回答结构： 承认问题复杂 + 可支持但不万能 + 协作转介。
I think Chinese Medicine may offer supportive value in areas such as sleep, stress regulation, and general wellbeing, but mental health should never be oversimplified. Some students may need counseling, psychiatric assessment, or multidisciplinary care. So I would see Chinese Medicine as a possible supportive part of a broader response rather than a stand-alone answer to everything. What matters most is taking distress seriously and helping young people access the right level of care early.
点评： 既看到中医可介入之处，也守住“不包打天下”的边界。

Q15. What role can this field play in ageing and chronic disease management?

考察点： 对香港医疗现实的理解。
回答结构： 慢病长期支持 + 功能改善 + 与主流系统配合。
In an ageing society, long-term symptom management, functional support, and quality of life become increasingly important. I think Chinese Medicine can play a meaningful role in chronic care, especially where patients need sustained support rather than only acute intervention. However, it should work alongside appropriate monitoring and referral systems, not outside them. Good chronic disease management is rarely about one heroic treatment. It is about continuity, coordination, and realistic support over time.
点评： 很好地把中医放进“continuity of care”框架，而不是孤立神化。

分组四：技术、跨文化与职业成长

Q16. How do you think about AI and digitalisation in Chinese Medicine?

考察点： 现代化意识。
回答结构： 可辅助 + 不取代临床关系 + 注意数据质量。
I think AI and digital tools can help with documentation, pattern analysis, research support, and possibly early risk detection, but they should remain assistive rather than authoritative. Chinese Medicine relies heavily on nuanced clinical observation and human interaction, so digitalisation should enhance practice rather than flatten it. I would also be careful about data quality and over-standardisation. If the input is shallow, the output may look precise but still mislead us.
点评： 回答既现代也不盲目，特别适合双学位申请者的气质。

Q17. How would you work in a multilingual clinical environment?

考察点： 语言与临床沟通适应力。
回答结构： clear communication + humility + checking understanding。
In a multilingual setting, I think clarity matters more than sounding impressive. I would focus on using language that the patient is most comfortable with, checking understanding regularly, and avoiding unnecessary jargon. If I am not fully fluent in a certain expression, I would rather clarify carefully than pretend. In healthcare, misunderstanding is not a small issue. Good communication is part of safety, not just courtesy.
点评： 把语言能力上升到 safety 层面，是非常成熟的医疗表达。

Q18. Tell us about a time you balanced science with empathy.

考察点： 人文与理性并重。
回答结构： 事件 + 你如何兼顾事实与感受 + 反思。
During a school wellbeing project, one teammate focused only on data collection, while another only cared about emotional storytelling. I tried to connect both by asking how we could use the data to identify real needs while still presenting students as people rather than statistics. That experience taught me that science gives structure, but empathy gives direction. In healthcare, I think both are necessary. Without science, care can become vague; without empathy, it can become cold.
点评： 这类答案最加分的是能自然落回 healthcare values。

Q19. How would you prepare for the Mainland internship and cross-border clinical exposure?

考察点： 对 programme reality 的理解。
回答结构： 心态准备 + 语言/文化适应 + 学习姿态。
I would prepare by treating the internship not as a formality but as a major learning transition. That means improving my Putonghua medical communication, understanding the clinical culture I am entering, and developing the humility to observe carefully before assuming I already know enough. I would also keep reflective notes, because clinical learning becomes deeper when experience is processed, not just accumulated. Cross-border exposure is demanding, but that is exactly why it is valuable.
点评： 体现出对实习强度与文化适应的真实理解，而不是泛泛说“我很期待”。

Q20. What kind of practitioner do you hope to become in ten years?

考察点： 长期愿景。
回答结构： 临床定位 + 跨体系沟通 + 人文目标。
In ten years, I hope to become a practitioner who is clinically grounded, scientifically literate, and deeply respectful toward patients. I want to be someone who can explain treatment clearly, recognize boundaries early, and work responsibly with other healthcare professionals when needed. Long term, I do not want to be known only for technical skill. I want to be trusted for judgment, sincerity, and the ability to care for people as whole human beings.
点评： 结尾落在 judgment、sincerity、whole human beings，非常适合本专业。
