专业面试训练

香港大学 JS6054 文学士

Bachelor of Arts（BA）

第一部分：JS6054 已知面试信息与考察逻辑

官方 / 校方可验证信息

  • 课程定位：HKU 官方 JUPAS 页面说明，JS6054 是一个四年制 Bachelor of Arts 课程，目标是让学生在 arts and humanities 范围内探索并拓展知识与经验。
  • Year 1 结构：官方明确把第一年定义为“Year of Exploration”。学生在第一年可追随自身兴趣，也要探索新的学科；并需修读至少 3 门 introductory Arts courses，通常包括未来主修或副修计划所需的先修课程。
  • 后续学术路径：官方说明学生在Year 1 结束后选择至少一个 Arts major；Year 2 至 Year 4 可继续配置额外的 Arts / non-Arts major，以及最多两个 minors，体现课程高度自由与规划能力要求。
  • 体验式学习：JUPAS 页面与 HKU Arts 官方本科页面均强调，课程中有丰富的internships、studying abroad、field trips、student exchange 等机会。
  • 面试邀请规则：HKU Arts BA admissions standards 页面写明：Selected candidates、non-school candidates，以及 candidates who have obtained level 3 in HKDSE English Language may be interviewed，并会按个案考虑。
  • 面试时间窗口：HKU JUPAS interview schedule 页面列出，JS6054 BA interview 在 7 月 22–23 日；并注明selected candidates and candidates with level 3 in HKDSE English Language may be invited to interview。
  • 公开信息边界：本次可核实的官方页面没有清楚写出 JS6054 的公开面试模式与面试语言，因此本页不把 mode / language 当成已确认事实。

高概率考察逻辑

  • Why Arts, not a narrow vocational subject：由于 BA 本身是 broad-based liberal arts programme，面试更可能看学生是否理解文科训练的价值：阅读、思辨、比较、表达、跨文化理解，而不是只会说“我喜欢文学”。
  • Can you use freedom well：Year 1 是探索年，之后可灵活配置 major / minor，因此面试官很可能关注学生是否具备自我规划能力，不会把“自由”理解成“没有方向”。
  • Intellectual curiosity：课程适合喜欢阅读、观察社会、思考文化与历史、愿意比较不同观点的学生，所以高概率会看学生是否有真实的求知欲与问题意识。
  • Breadth + focus：HKU BA 既鼓励探索，也要求学生能逐步形成主线，因此回答时最好体现“我开放，但不是空泛”，即：有初步兴趣，同时愿意在大学中继续验证与调整。
  • Expression and reflection：文学院面试通常很看重表达、反思、例证与逻辑组织能力。即使题目不难，若学生回答空泛、概念堆砌、没有例子，就容易失分。
  • Global outlook：官方不断强调 exchange、field trips 和 study abroad，说明 programme 很重视全球视野与文化理解；这类题型很适合在训练中重点准备。

训练建议：HKU BA 最忌讳“我喜欢看书 / 我对文科有兴趣”式的松散表达。老师训练时应把学生回答推进到：一个真实的兴趣主题 + 你为什么对它有问题意识 + HKU BA 的探索性与灵活性如何帮助你发展。此外，由于官方写明部分 level 3 英文考生也可能被邀请面试，因此英文表达准备仍然值得重视，但本页不会把英文面试写成已确认事实。

第二部分：JS6054 高频问题库与参考答案（20题）

分组一：专业动机与课程匹配

Q1. Why do you want to study a Bachelor of Arts?

考察点：是否真正理解文科训练。
回答结构：兴趣主题 + 文科方法 + 长期价值。
I want to study a Bachelor of Arts because I am interested not only in learning facts, but also in understanding how people, cultures, ideas, and histories shape the world. What attracts me to Arts is its way of training students to read carefully, think critically, compare perspectives, and communicate clearly. I see it as a discipline that helps students make sense of complex human questions rather than looking for only one fixed answer. That kind of intellectual training is meaningful to me, and I believe it will also remain valuable far beyond university.
点评：重点是把 BA 说成一种思维训练，而不是泛泛的“喜欢文科”。

Q2. Why HKU BA instead of choosing a more specialised programme immediately?

考察点：是否理解 broad-based 课程结构。
回答结构：探索年价值 + 当前兴趣 + 更成熟选择。
I think HKU BA suits me because it offers both freedom and structure. I already have some areas that interest me, but I also know that a strong university education should allow room for exploration before a final academic path is fixed. HKU’s Year of Exploration is attractive because it encourages students to test their interests through actual courses rather than relying only on assumptions made before entering university. I see that flexibility as a strength, because it allows students to make more mature and informed choices later.
点评：这题要把“先 broad、后聚焦”说成理性选择，不是没有方向。

Q3. What kinds of questions or topics naturally attract you?

考察点：有没有真实的学术兴趣。
回答结构：主题 + 为什么吸引你 + 它反映什么思维倾向。
I am naturally drawn to questions that involve interpretation rather than only memorisation. For example, I am interested in how language shapes thought, how history influences identity, and how cultural differences affect the way people understand the same issue. What I enjoy is that these topics rarely have one simple answer. They require evidence, comparison, and reflection. I think that is one reason the Arts appeal to me so strongly, because they train students to stay thoughtful when dealing with complexity.
点评：比“我喜欢中文/英文/历史”更强，因为体现了问题意识。

Q4. What do you think Arts education can offer that is still important today?

考察点：是否能为文科价值辩护。
回答结构：信息时代 + 判断力 + 表达与理解。
I think Arts education remains important because having access to information is not the same as having judgment. In a world full of fast opinions, AI-generated content, and constant information flow, people still need the ability to interpret ideas, evaluate arguments, understand cultural contexts, and communicate responsibly. Arts education develops those abilities. It does not only teach content. It trains habits of mind that help people think more carefully and respond more intelligently to change.
点评：这是港大 BA 很适合准备的一类价值型问题。

Q5. How would you explain your interest in Arts to someone who thinks it is too broad?

考察点：能否把“广”说成优势。
回答结构： broad 不是 vague，而是 foundational and flexible。
I would say that broad does not mean directionless. It means building a strong foundation before specialising too narrowly. In the Arts, broad exposure helps students discover how different disciplines connect, whether through language, history, philosophy, culture, or society. That kind of training can actually make later choices stronger, because students are not choosing blindly. They are choosing after exploring. So I see breadth not as weakness, but as intellectual preparation for more meaningful focus later.
点评：非常贴合 HKU BA 的 programme structure。

分组二：探索年、学术规划与阅读思维

Q6. How would you make good use of the Year of Exploration?

考察点：是否具备 self-directed learning 意识。
回答结构：探索不是乱选课，而是有目标地测试兴趣。
I would use the Year of Exploration to test my interests systematically rather than casually. I would choose some courses that connect to what I already enjoy, but I would also deliberately take at least one or two subjects that challenge my assumptions about myself. My goal would be to observe not only what I like, but also where I think well, what kinds of texts or questions energise me, and what I would want to study more seriously in later years. I think exploration is most useful when it is reflective and intentional.
点评：这题很关键，港大 BA 很看重学生会不会使用自由度。

Q7. If you could currently imagine a possible major or minor, what might it be?

考察点：有没有初步方向。
回答结构：当前偏好 + 理由 + 保留开放性。
At the moment, I could imagine myself leaning toward areas such as history, literature, linguistics, or global and area studies, depending on how my first year develops. These areas appeal to me because they all involve interpretation, context, and the relationship between ideas and society. At the same time, I appreciate that HKU BA does not require students to finalise everything too early. I think it is mature to have a direction while still being open to refining it through actual learning.
点评：老师可按学生实际兴趣改写，不必固定某一个 major。

Q8. What do you think makes a good reader or thinker?

考察点：能否抽象总结文科学习素养。
回答结构：耐心、证据、比较、反思。
I think a good reader or thinker is someone who is patient enough to go beyond first impressions. It is easy to react quickly, but much harder to read carefully, notice assumptions, compare interpretations, and ask what evidence really supports a claim. A good thinker is also willing to revise an opinion when a stronger argument appears. To me, that combination of curiosity, discipline, and humility is what makes intellectual work meaningful.
点评：很适合文学院面试，可看出学生气质与思维方式。

Q9. Tell us about a book, article, film, or idea that made you think more deeply.

考察点：是否有真实输入与反思。
回答结构：内容 + 触发的问题 + 你的变化。
One kind of experience that influences me strongly is when a text or film changes not only what I know, but how I ask questions. What stays with me is usually not a simple conclusion, but a shift in perspective. For example, when a work reveals how differently people can understand identity, history, or justice, it reminds me that interpretation matters. I value experiences like that because they make me more thoughtful and less superficial in the way I respond to ideas.
点评：正式训练时应换成学生自己的真实材料，不能空讲。

Q10. How do you handle a question that does not have one clear answer?

考察点：能否接受复杂性。
回答结构：先界定问题，再比较立场，再形成判断。
When a question does not have one clear answer, I try not to see that as a weakness of the question. Instead, I treat it as a sign that the issue requires more careful thinking. I would begin by clarifying the assumptions behind the question, then compare possible perspectives and ask what evidence or values support each one. I think mature thinking is not about pretending uncertainty does not exist. It is about learning how to reason responsibly within uncertainty.
点评：这题非常适合 BA，因为很多 humanities 问题本就不求单一答案。

分组三：全球视野、体验式学习与现实关联

Q11. Why do you think exchange or study abroad matters for Arts students?

考察点：是否理解官方强调的 global experience。
回答结构：跨文化理解 + 视角变化 + 自我成长。
I think exchange matters for Arts students because many Arts questions involve culture, language, identity, and interpretation. These things become more meaningful when students encounter different contexts directly rather than only reading about them. Exchange can challenge assumptions, improve cultural sensitivity, and make students more aware of how differently people experience the world. That kind of growth is academically valuable, but it is also personally transformative.
点评：这题与 HKU Arts 官方反复强调的 exchange positioning 非常吻合。

Q12. What can internships or field trips contribute to an Arts education?

考察点：是否把文科和现实世界连接起来。
回答结构：课堂与现实互证。
Internships and field trips help Arts students connect abstract ideas with real contexts. In class, students may study culture, institutions, language, or history in conceptual ways. Practical experiences allow them to see how those ideas operate in organisations, communities, or public life. I think this matters because Arts education should not remain purely theoretical. The best learning often happens when reflection and experience strengthen each other.
点评：能有效纠正文科=只读书的刻板印象。

Q13. How do you think Arts graduates can contribute to society or the workplace?

考察点：是否理解 BA 的现实价值。
回答结构：分析、沟通、写作、判断、跨文化能力。
I think Arts graduates can contribute in many ways because they are often trained to analyse complexity, communicate clearly, work with ideas responsibly, and understand people across different contexts. Those strengths matter in education, media, policy, business, cultural work, international settings, and many other fields. The value of an Arts graduate is not limited to one profession. It lies in the ability to think well, write well, adapt well, and bring perspective into decision-making.
点评：回答要有现实感，不要只谈理想化的人文价值。

Q14. What social or cultural issue are you currently paying attention to?

考察点：是否对现实世界保持关注。
回答结构：议题 + 为什么值得关注 + 你在思考什么。
I am interested in social or cultural issues that show how values, identity, and communication interact. What matters to me is not only the headline of an issue, but the deeper question beneath it: how people interpret fairness, belonging, language, memory, or responsibility differently. I think Arts training is valuable because it teaches students to move from reaction to interpretation. That is the kind of deeper engagement I hope to keep developing.
点评：老师实际带练时应替换成学生真的长期关注的话题。

Q15. Why might flexibility in major and minor choices be a strength rather than a problem?

考察点：能否正确理解 curriculum flexibility。
回答结构：允许连接兴趣、形成个人学术路径。
Flexibility can be a strength because it allows students to build a more meaningful academic path instead of following a rigid template that may not fit them well. In a programme like BA, students often discover that their interests are connected rather than isolated. Being able to combine majors or minors can help them create an education that is both coherent and personal. Of course, flexibility also requires responsibility, which is why planning and reflection are so important.
点评：这题能直接考学生对 HKU BA 课程结构的理解。

分组四：表达能力、反思能力与面试表现

Q16. Please introduce yourself briefly.

考察点：开场表达是否清楚、有气质。
回答结构：身份 + intellectual curiosity + why BA。
Good afternoon, and thank you for the opportunity. I am a student who is deeply interested in how ideas, cultures, and human experiences can be understood through careful reading, reflection, and discussion. Over time, I realised that what attracts me most is not only learning information, but learning how to interpret it thoughtfully. That is why I am applying to HKU’s Bachelor of Arts programme. I value its Year of Exploration, its flexibility, and the way it encourages students to develop both breadth and depth over time.
点评：很适合作为 BA 页面的通用英文开场模板。

Q17. How would you respond if someone disagreed strongly with your interpretation?

考察点：是否有讨论素养。
回答结构：先听，再比较理由，再修正或坚持。
If someone strongly disagreed with my interpretation, I would first try to understand the reasoning behind their view instead of defending mine immediately. In the Arts, disagreement is often productive because it can reveal different assumptions or evidence that I may not have considered. I would explain my view clearly, but I would also be open to revising it if the other argument was stronger. To me, good discussion is not about winning. It is about reaching a deeper understanding.
点评：文学院很重视这种讨论气质与 intellectual humility。

Q18. How do you usually form your own opinion on a complicated issue?

考察点：判断过程是否成熟。
回答结构：多看资料 + 分辨观点 + 形成暂时判断。
When I form an opinion on a complicated issue, I try to avoid reacting too quickly. I usually begin by understanding the background, then I compare different viewpoints and ask what assumptions or evidence each side relies on. After that, I form a judgment, but I see it as a reasoned position rather than something fixed forever. I think that approach is important because complicated issues often require both conviction and openness.
点评：很适合 humanities 面试，可看出学生的思维过程。

Q19. What would you do if university-level reading or writing felt more difficult than expected?

考察点：学习韧性。
回答结构：承认挑战 + 调整方法 + 主动求助。
If university-level reading or writing felt more difficult than expected, I would treat that as part of the transition rather than as proof that I did not belong. I would adjust my methods, ask for feedback, and work more deliberately on how I read, take notes, and structure arguments. Difficulties do not worry me as much as staying passive. I think students grow most when they respond to challenge with discipline and reflection instead of avoidance.
点评：能体现成熟度，比空喊“我会努力”更有效。

Q20. What kind of student do you hope to become in HKU BA?

考察点：是否有成长目标。
回答结构：学习者气质 + 学术能力 + 全球视野。
I hope to become a student who is intellectually curious, disciplined, and open-minded. I want to grow into someone who reads more carefully, writes more clearly, and thinks with greater independence and responsibility. I also hope to make use of the opportunities in HKU BA, such as interdisciplinary study and global exposure, to broaden my perspective. More than simply collecting credentials, I want to build a way of thinking that will stay valuable throughout my life.
点评：收束性很好，适合用作结尾类问题训练。
