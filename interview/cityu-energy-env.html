香港城市大学 JS1051 能源及环境学

Energy and Environment（Majors: BEng Energy Science and Engineering / BEng Environmental Science and Engineering）

第一部分：JS1051 已知面试形式与考察逻辑

官方 / 校方可验证信息

  • 面试安排：CityU 官方 Interview Arrangements 页面写明，JS1051 为 selective basis，时间在 early to mid-July 2026，形式为 group interview，语言为 English。
  • 补充细节：School of Energy and Environment 的 admission arrangements 页面进一步写明，面试会在 HKDSE 放榜前的 2026 年 7 月进行，模式为 group interview only，时长约 30 minutes。
  • 项目结构：JS1051 是 Flexible Pathway。学生先修读一年的 common foundation study，之后再在BEng Energy Science and Engineering 与 BEng Environmental Science and Engineering 两个主修之间作出选择。
  • 基础训练：官方 programme page 强调 Year 1 会先建立 broad scientific foundation，涵盖mathematics, computer science, physics, chemistry and biology，再深入能源与环境议题，说明面试官很可能看重学生是否接受跨学科训练。
  • 项目定位：School of Energy and Environment 以解决energy crisis, renewable energy, global warming, climate change and pollution 为核心，并强调为 rapidly decarbonizing world 培养专业人才。
  • 专业认证：BEng Energy Science and Engineering 获 HKIE Energy and Environmental Disciplines 认证；BEng Environmental Science and Engineering 获 HKIE Environmental Discipline 认证。
  • 主修差异：ESE 更强调能源系统、能源安全、可再生能源、热流体、能源政策与工程技术；EVE 更强调 environmental technology、sustainability and environmental management、environmental science 三大训练方向。

高概率考察逻辑

  • Why sustainability now：这不是单纯“喜欢环保”即可过关的专业。老师要训练学生把兴趣说到能源转型、气候变化、污染治理、资源效率与工程方案层面。
  • Why JS1051 instead of a narrow single-major choice：由于 JS1051 是先 foundation 再选主修，面试官大概率会看学生是否理解 flexible pathway 的价值，并能解释自己为何暂时不急于锁定单一方向。
  • Science + Engineering + Society：官方定位本身就是跨学科，因此很可能考查学生能否把科学知识、工程方案、政策/社会影响连起来讲，而不是只停留在口号式 ESG 表达。
  • English group discussion：官方明确是英文群面，30 分钟只做 group interview，因此表达条理、回应别人、推动讨论、把复杂问题讲清楚，会比背知识点更重要。
  • Problem-solving orientation：从课程与专业认证可看出，这个项目要的不只是“有环保意识”的学生，而是愿意用数据、系统、工程设计去解决真实问题的学生。

训练建议：JS1051 的回答要避免“环保很好、我想帮助地球”这种空泛表达。更强的方式是：现实问题场景 + 你对能源或环境系统的具体兴趣 + 你希望在 CityU 获得的跨学科训练。老师带练时要特别强化学生的英文群面表达、系统性解释能力，以及“能源”和“环境”两个方向的初步辨析能力。

第二部分：JS1051 高频问题库与参考答案（20题）

分组一：专业动机与项目匹配

Q1. Why are you interested in Energy and Environment as a field of study?

考察点：是否能把兴趣落到真实议题。
回答结构：现实问题 + 个人关注 + 工程视角。
I am interested in Energy and Environment because it focuses on problems that are both urgent and deeply connected to everyday life. Issues such as climate change, energy security, pollution, and sustainable development are no longer distant topics. They affect cities, industries, and public health in very practical ways. What attracts me most is that this field does not only describe the problems. It trains students to think about technical and system-level solutions. I want to study a subject where science, engineering, and social impact are closely connected, and this programme fits that goal very well.
点评：重点不是单纯“我关心地球”，而是把议题说成需要工程解决的问题。

Q2. Why did you choose JS1051 instead of applying only for a single specialised major?

考察点：是否理解 flexible pathway。
回答结构：先探索 + 有初步方向 + 愿意用 foundation 验证。
I chose JS1051 because I value the flexibility of building a strong foundation before deciding between the two majors. At this stage, I am genuinely interested in both the energy side and the environmental side, and I think it is wiser to test that interest through actual first-year study rather than forcing an early decision based only on impressions. The programme structure allows me to strengthen my background in science and engineering first, then make a more informed choice. To me, that is not indecision. It is a disciplined way to choose a direction with better understanding.
点评：这题一定要把“先基础、后分流”说成成熟选择，而不是没有方向。

Q3. At the moment, which major interests you more: Energy Science and Engineering or Environmental Science and Engineering?

考察点：是否有当前偏好。
回答结构：当前偏好 + 理由 + 保留开放度。
At the moment, I am slightly more interested in Energy Science and Engineering because I am drawn to questions about energy transition, renewable systems, and how engineering can improve energy efficiency in the real world. At the same time, I also recognise that environmental science and engineering is closely connected to sustainability, pollution control, and resource management, which are equally important. That is why I see JS1051 as a good fit. I can enter with a current preference, but still let my first-year learning experience refine that choice.
点评：回答里有“当前偏好 + 仍理解另一方向价值”会更稳。

Q4. Why do you think this field matters especially now?

考察点：对时代背景的理解。
回答结构：能源转型 + 气候挑战 + 工程人才需求。
I think this field matters especially now because the world is under pressure to decarbonise while still meeting growing energy needs. Societies cannot simply choose between development and sustainability. They need better systems that make both possible. At the same time, problems such as pollution, climate risk, and resource constraints are becoming more visible and more costly. That means future engineers need to understand not only how systems work, but also how they affect the environment and how they can be improved. This field prepares students for exactly that challenge.
点评：很适合训练学生把“时代大背景”讲得简洁而不空泛。

Q5. What kind of impact would you like to make through this discipline?

考察点：价值取向与职业想象。
回答结构：影响对象 + 方式 + 长期方向。
I would like to contribute to solutions that are practical, scalable, and meaningful rather than only idealistic. Whether that means improving energy efficiency, supporting cleaner technologies, or helping systems become more sustainable, I want my work to create measurable improvement in how people live and how industries operate. I do not expect to change everything at once, but I do hope to become someone who can connect technical knowledge with real implementation. To me, meaningful impact in this field comes from combining responsibility with competence.
点评：比“改变世界”更稳妥，强调可执行和可衡量的影响。

分组二：学科理解与系统思维

Q6. What is the difference between solving an environmental problem scientifically and solving it through engineering?

考察点：是否理解 science 与 engineering 的区别。
回答结构：解释问题 vs 设计方案。
Scientific thinking helps us understand what is happening and why. It focuses on explanation, evidence, and the mechanisms behind a problem. Engineering goes one step further by asking how to design a workable solution under real constraints such as cost, scale, safety, and efficiency. In environmental and energy issues, both are essential. We need science to understand climate processes, pollution pathways, or energy systems, but we also need engineering to turn that understanding into technologies, infrastructure, and policies that can actually be implemented.
点评：这是 JS1051 很典型的核心题，能检验学生是否真的理解学科定位。

Q7. Why is a broad foundation in mathematics, physics, chemistry, biology, and computer science useful for this programme?

考察点：是否接受跨学科训练。
回答结构：复杂问题需要多种工具。
A broad foundation is useful because energy and environmental problems rarely stay within one discipline. Mathematics helps with modelling and analysis. Physics is important for energy systems, thermodynamics, and transport processes. Chemistry is essential for reactions, materials, and pollution treatment. Biology matters in ecosystems, environmental processes, and some treatment systems. Computer science is increasingly important for simulation, monitoring, optimisation, and data analysis. In a field like this, having only one lens would be too limiting. A broad foundation helps students understand systems more completely and make better decisions later.
点评：回答要体现“复杂问题不能只靠单一学科”。

Q8. What do you think is the relationship between energy transition and environmental protection?

考察点：能否把两个关键词连起来。
回答结构：相互支持，但也有 trade-offs。
I think the relationship is close but not always simple. Energy transition is often necessary for environmental protection because cleaner energy systems can reduce emissions and support long-term sustainability. However, every transition also involves trade-offs in land use, materials, cost, and infrastructure. That means the goal is not only to replace one energy source with another, but to design systems that are cleaner, efficient, and realistic at scale. So I see the relationship as one of mutual support, but also one that requires careful engineering judgment.
点评：答出“支持关系 + 现实权衡”会显得成熟很多。

Q9. What makes sustainability a technical challenge rather than only a moral idea?

考察点：能否避免空泛 ESG 语言。
回答结构：目标需要系统设计与量化。
Sustainability is often discussed as a value, but to achieve it in practice we need technical systems that actually work. Cleaner production, energy efficiency, pollution control, waste treatment, and resource management all require engineering methods, measurement, and optimisation. If sustainability stays only at the level of moral intention, it may sound inspiring but remain ineffective. What makes it meaningful is the ability to translate values into designs, standards, and operating systems that produce real results.
点评：非常适合训练学生把“理念”转成“工程行动”。

Q10. How would you explain the difference between an energy problem and an environmental problem?

考察点：概念辨析。
回答结构：关注重点不同，但高度关联。
An energy problem usually focuses on how energy is produced, stored, delivered, and used efficiently and securely. An environmental problem focuses more on impacts such as pollution, ecosystem stress, emissions, and long-term sustainability. However, in reality the two are deeply connected. Energy choices can create environmental consequences, while environmental constraints can reshape energy systems. That is why I think this programme is attractive. It trains students to understand both sides instead of treating them as separate worlds.
点评：老师可用这题训练学生做清晰概念区分。

分组三：方向选择、现实议题与资源利用

Q11. If you later chose Energy Science and Engineering, what would attract you most about it?

考察点：是否理解 ESE 的方向。
回答结构：能源系统 + 技术兴趣 + 现实价值。
If I later chose Energy Science and Engineering, what would attract me most is its focus on how society can move toward cleaner and more efficient energy systems without losing reliability. I am especially interested in the challenge of balancing technology, infrastructure, and real-world constraints. Topics like renewable energy, energy efficiency, energy conversion, and system optimisation appeal to me because they are both technically demanding and socially important. I like the idea of working on solutions that support long-term energy security while also reducing environmental pressure.
点评：适合训练学生把 ESE 讲成“系统工程方向”，而非只会说太阳能。

Q12. If you later chose Environmental Science and Engineering, what would attract you most about it?

考察点：是否理解 EVE 的方向。
回答结构：环境系统 + 治理思路 + 专业责任感。
If I later chose Environmental Science and Engineering, I would be most attracted to its combination of science, engineering, and sustainability. I am interested in how environmental professionals analyse problems such as pollution, waste, or environmental risk and then design systems that improve performance in measurable ways. The programme’s emphasis on environmental technology, sustainability and management, and environmental science makes it appealing because it does not treat the environment as an abstract topic. It approaches it as a field where evidence, design, and responsibility must work together.
点评：要体现 EVE 的专业性，不只是“喜欢自然”。

Q13. How would you make use of CityU’s interdisciplinary environment if you entered JS1051?

考察点：是否会主动使用项目资源。
回答结构：先打基础 + 再结合项目/研究/交流。
If I entered JS1051, I would first focus on making the most of the foundation year because that is where I can build the scientific and analytical habits that support later specialisation. After that, I would try to use the interdisciplinary environment to test my interests more actively—through projects, discussion with teachers, and opportunities related to sustainability or applied engineering. What attracts me is that the programme is not narrow. It creates room for students to connect technical learning with broader environmental and societal questions. I would want to use that space to become a more thoughtful and adaptable engineer.
点评：这题适合把“资源利用意识”练出来，不要只会说会努力读书。

Q14. What do you think is harder: inventing a greener technology or getting society to adopt it?

考察点：对现实落地的理解。
回答结构：技术与采用都难，说明原因。
I think both are difficult, but in many cases adoption is harder because even a good technology must fit economic, behavioural, and regulatory realities. A solution may be technically strong, but if it is too costly, too complex, or poorly integrated into existing systems, it may not achieve large-scale impact. At the same time, adoption without sound technology is also risky. That is why I think this field needs people who understand both technical design and real-world implementation. True progress happens when good ideas can survive practical conditions.
点评：这是很好的群面延展题，能看学生是否理解“落地难度”。

Q15. What kind of career path do you currently imagine for yourself in this field?

考察点：职业方向是否合理。
回答结构：先 broad vision，再保留成长空间。
At the moment, I imagine myself working in a role where technical knowledge can directly support sustainable systems, whether in engineering design, environmental consulting, energy-related projects, or analytical work connected to decarbonisation. I am still open to where I may eventually specialise, which is one reason this programme suits me. What matters most to me now is building the competence to work on real problems rather than choosing a job title too early. I would rather grow into a role through strong training than force a narrow career plan before I have the necessary experience.
点评：既有职业感，又不过度承诺具体职位，适合高中生表达。

分组四：英文群面、沟通与成长潜力

Q16. Please introduce yourself briefly.

考察点：英文开场是否清楚。
回答结构：身份 + 关注议题 + why JS1051。
Good afternoon, and thank you for the opportunity. I am a student who is strongly interested in how science and engineering can be used to solve real problems related to sustainability, energy, and the environment. Over time, I realised that I am not only concerned about these issues in a general sense. I want to understand them more deeply and learn how technical systems can improve them. That is why I am applying to CityU’s JS1051 programme. I value its interdisciplinary foundation and the chance to explore both energy and environmental engineering before choosing a final major.
点评：非常适合作为英文群面开场模板，结构简洁稳定。

Q17. In a group discussion, how would you contribute if the topic was unfamiliar to you?

考察点：群面应对能力。
回答结构：先听懂框架，再总结、提问、推进。
If the topic was unfamiliar to me, I would first listen carefully and try to identify the structure of the discussion rather than panic. Even if I did not know every technical detail, I could still contribute by clarifying the question, comparing options logically, summarising what others said, or asking useful questions that move the discussion forward. I think group discussion is not only about who knows the most facts. It is also about whether you can stay calm, think clearly, and help the group become more organised and productive.
点评：很适合训练学生在陌生题群面中保持有效输出。

Q18. How would you explain a sustainability issue to someone who thinks it is only a political slogan?

考察点：解释力与说服力。
回答结构：从现实系统和成本角度解释。
I would explain that sustainability becomes real when it affects how systems are designed, operated, and maintained. It is not only about political language. It is about whether energy use is efficient, whether pollution is controlled, whether resources are wasted, and whether long-term costs are being ignored. In that sense, sustainability is closely linked to engineering and management decisions. If we treat it only as a slogan, we miss the fact that it has direct technical and economic consequences.
点评：可训练学生把抽象概念讲成现实系统问题。

Q19. Tell us about a time you changed your mind after learning more about a problem.

考察点：学习能力与开放度。
回答结构：原判断 + 新信息 + 修正过程。
One important lesson I have learned is that complex problems often look simple only from a distance. There have been situations where I formed an initial opinion quickly, but after reading more or hearing different perspectives, I realised the issue had more constraints and trade-offs than I first thought. Instead of seeing that as inconsistency, I see it as growth. In a field like energy and environment, being willing to update your view when the evidence changes is a strength, because real-world problems are rarely one-dimensional.
点评：很符合可持续与系统问题的学科气质，强调 evidence-based thinking。

Q20. What would you do if, after entering JS1051, your first preferred major changed?

考察点：适应力与决策成熟度。
回答结构：把调整视为 programme value，而非失败。
If my first preferred major changed after entering JS1051, I would not see that as failure. I would see it as evidence that the flexible structure of the programme had done its job. The purpose of the foundation year is not only to teach content, but also to help students understand their own strengths and interests more realistically. If my direction changed, I would make the new decision based on what I had learned from coursework, reflection, and experience. I think a thoughtful adjustment is often wiser than holding onto an early assumption just to appear consistent.
点评：这是 JS1051 页面必须训练的一题，和项目结构高度贴合。
