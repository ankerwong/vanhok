PolyU 应用数学及金融分析

量化思维、风险与回报、FinTech 与 AI 题库。

第四部分：PolyU 应用数学及金融分析——已知面试形式与考察逻辑

官方确立规则

  • 面试安排： 采取筛选性面试（Suitable candidates may be invited for interviews）。
  • 语言与形式： 面试以英文为主，常见为个人面试，亦可能以 Zoom interview 形式进行。
  • 时长： 官方公开信息显示约 20-30 分钟。
  • 考察目标： 重点评估申请者对课程的兴趣与潜力，以及 communication skills 和 programme-related knowledge。
  • 课程结构： 设 Common Year One，之后可在 Applied Mathematics、Investment Science and Finance Analytics、Quantitative Finance and FinTech 等方向中发展。
  • 项目训练重点： 数学、统计、编程与金融分析交叉并重，强调 quantitative analysis、critical thinking、problem-solving 与 communication。

高概率考察逻辑： 这类面试通常不会只问“你数学好不好”，而更关注你是否真正理解数学如何在金融、风险、数据分析与决策中创造价值。高频追问往往包括：为什么不是纯数学或纯金融、如何看待模型与现实之间的偏差、如何用通俗语言解释 quantitative thinking、是否具备对不确定性与数据伦理的边界意识，以及你能否把抽象分析落到清晰表达与团队协作上。

第九部分：PolyU 应用数学及金融分析 高频问题库与参考答案（20题）

分组一：专业动机与项目匹配

Q1. Why this programme instead of pure finance or pure mathematics?

考察点： 对交叉学科定位的理解。
回答结构： 纯金融的局限 + 纯数学的局限 + 你想要的结合点。
I am drawn to this programme because I do not want to choose between abstraction and application too early. Pure mathematics gives powerful ways of thinking, while finance provides fast-moving real-world problems involving risk, uncertainty, and decision-making. What attracts me here is the bridge between the two. I want rigorous quantitative training, but I also want to see how models behave when they meet noisy data, changing markets, and human behavior. To me, that combination is more intellectually honest and more professionally useful than studying either side in isolation.
点评： 不是简单说“我两个都喜欢”，而是明确指出 bridge 的价值，成熟度很高。

Q2. Why PolyU for Applied Mathematics and Finance Analytics?

考察点： 对课程结构和资源的了解。
回答结构： Common Year One + 数学统计编程基础 + 金融分析应用。
PolyU attracts me because the programme is structured in a way that builds depth before specialization. The Common Year One is important to me because I want a stronger foundation in mathematics, statistics, and programming before deciding how far I lean toward applied mathematics, finance analytics, or fintech. I also like that the programme is clearly application-oriented. It is not just about learning formulas, but about using quantitative tools to solve real financial and analytical problems. That balance of rigor and applicability is exactly what I am looking for.
点评： 把 Common Year One 说成优势而不是不确定性，体现出清晰的学习策略。

Q3. Which direction are you most interested in after the common first year?

考察点： 自我认知与探索意识。
回答结构： 当前倾向 + 理由 + 保留开放性。
At this stage, I am most interested in Investment Science and Finance Analytics, because I am fascinated by how quantitative reasoning can support investment decisions under uncertainty. I enjoy the idea of combining probability, statistics, and modelling with practical financial questions. At the same time, I appreciate that good analytical judgment requires strong mathematical fundamentals, so I do not see the first year as a waiting room. I see it as the stage where I build the language and discipline needed to make a smarter choice later.
点评： 既有方向，又不过度僵化，适合这种带分流机制的课程面试。

Q4. What does quantitative thinking mean to you?

考察点： 是否理解 quantitative thinking 的本质。
回答结构： 定义 + 假设 + 证据 + 反思。
Quantitative thinking, to me, means turning a vague question into a structured one. It starts with defining the problem clearly, identifying relevant variables, making assumptions explicit, and then testing whether the evidence actually supports the conclusion. It is not the same as worshipping numbers. Good quantitative thinking also asks whether the data are biased, whether the model is too simplified, and whether the result makes sense in context. So I see it as disciplined reasoning rather than just calculation.
点评： 这类回答最加分的地方在于：你没有把 quantitative 理解成“会算题”。

Q5. Tell us about a time numbers changed your decision.

考察点： 数据意识与真实应用能力。
回答结构： 原本直觉 + 数据分析 + 决策改变。
During a school fundraising event, our team initially wanted to print a large number of posters because we assumed visibility was the main problem. Before spending the budget, I compared response rates from previous events and noticed that online sign-ups driven by targeted class messages had converted much better than physical posters. That changed our plan. We reduced print spending and shifted effort toward more focused digital outreach. The event still reached a strong turnout, and our cost efficiency improved. That experience taught me that data does not remove judgment, but it can prevent lazy assumptions.
点评： 用小场景讲出 data-driven decision，很自然，也体现了资源配置意识。

分组二：数学、风险与金融理解

Q6. How would you explain risk and return to a younger student?

考察点： 概念理解与通俗表达能力。
回答结构： 先定义，再用生活化类比，最后补边界。
I would say return is what you hope to gain, while risk is how uncertain that gain is. A simple way to explain it is to compare lending money to a very reliable friend versus investing in a new business idea. The new business might give you a much bigger reward, but the outcome is less predictable, so the risk is higher. However, high risk does not automatically mean high return. It only means the range of possible outcomes is wider. Good decision-making is not about chasing the biggest number. It is about asking whether the possible reward is worth the uncertainty.
点评： 解释清楚，而且主动加上“高风险不等于高回报”，这是成熟表达。

Q7. Why are probabilities often more useful than certainties in finance?

考察点： 对不确定性的认知。
回答结构： 现实不确定 + 概率帮助决策 + 不等于保证。
Finance deals with uncertain systems shaped by information, incentives, and human behavior, so certainty is often an illusion. Probabilities are useful because they force us to think in ranges and scenarios rather than pretending we know one exact future. That matters for pricing, portfolio construction, and risk management. For example, an investor does not need a perfect forecast to make a better decision. They need a realistic view of likely outcomes, downside exposure, and how different assumptions change the picture. Probability is not weakness. It is a more honest language for uncertainty.
点评： 用“honest language for uncertainty”来收束，非常适合量化面试语境。

Q8. What is volatility, and what does it not tell us?

考察点： 概念辨析与边界意识。
回答结构： 定义波动率 + 功能 + 局限。
Volatility measures how much prices fluctuate over time, so it gives us a sense of how unstable or variable an asset has been. It is useful because large price swings usually imply greater uncertainty and risk for planning. But volatility does not tell us everything. It does not explain why prices are moving, whether a move is fundamentally justified, or whether extreme events are becoming more likely. It also treats upside and downside movement similarly, even though investors usually worry more about downside pain. So volatility is useful, but it is not a complete definition of risk.
点评： 这类题高分关键在后半句：知道它有用，也知道它不够用。

Q9. Can financial models predict markets accurately?

考察点： 对模型能力与局限的判断。
回答结构： 模型有用 + 模型不完美 + 关键在用途。
Financial models can be very useful, but I would be careful with the word predict. In my view, models are better at organizing information, comparing scenarios, and quantifying trade-offs than at forecasting markets with precision. Markets are influenced by policy shocks, sentiment, liquidity, and behavior that can shift suddenly. So a model can improve discipline, but it cannot eliminate uncertainty. I think the real value of a model lies in helping people ask better questions, stress-test assumptions, and avoid purely emotional decisions.
点评： 没有迷信模型，也没有否定模型，态度非常稳。

Q10. What makes a good finance analyst?

考察点： 职业理解是否成熟。
回答结构： 分析能力 + 判断力 + 沟通能力。
A good finance analyst needs more than technical skill. First, they need analytical discipline: the ability to work carefully with data, assumptions, and logic. Second, they need judgment, because even strong analysis can be misleading if the wrong question is being asked. Third, they need communication skills, since a good analysis is useless if decision-makers cannot understand its implications. I also think intellectual humility matters. In finance, overconfidence can be more dangerous than incomplete knowledge. A strong analyst should be rigorous, but also willing to revise a view when the evidence changes.
点评： 把 analyst 说成“分析 + 判断 + 表达”的组合，而不是只会算数，很专业。

分组三：数据、AI 与 FinTech

Q11. What excites you about FinTech right now?

考察点： 对行业前沿的关注度。
回答结构： 一个具体变化 + 它为什么重要 + 你的兴趣点。
What excites me most is how FinTech makes financial services more programmable, measurable, and accessible. I am especially interested in how data infrastructure and automation improve decision speed in areas like fraud detection, risk monitoring, and personalized financial services. What I find most compelling is not technology for its own sake, but the fact that it changes how financial systems operate in real time. At the same time, it raises important questions about transparency and fairness, which makes the field both technically challenging and socially relevant.
点评： 不是泛泛讲区块链或 AI，而是抓住“programmable, measurable, accessible”。

Q12. How is AI changing finance analytics?

考察点： 对 AI 赋能与风险的平衡理解。
回答结构： 效率提升 + 新能力 + 风险边界。
AI is changing finance analytics by improving pattern detection, automating repetitive analysis, and allowing institutions to process much larger volumes of data. It can support credit scoring, anomaly detection, sentiment analysis, and faster scenario exploration. However, I do not think AI removes the need for human judgment. Financial data is shaped by regulation, incentives, and sudden regime shifts, so a model that performs well historically may fail in a new context. That is why I see AI as a powerful analytical partner, but not an excuse to stop questioning assumptions.
点评： 和医学、营销部分保持一致：承认 AI 很强，但最终还是要人来负责判断。

Q13. What are the dangers of relying too much on historical data?

考察点： 对数据局限与 regime change 的理解。
回答结构： 历史数据有价值 + 结构变化风险 + 应对方式。
Historical data is essential, but it can become dangerous when people treat the past as a stable blueprint for the future. Markets change because incentives, regulations, technology, and behavior change. A model trained on historical data may perform well in familiar conditions and then fail when the environment shifts. There is also a risk of mistaking correlation for causation. So I think historical data should guide analysis, but not imprison it. Good analysts should combine data with stress testing, scenario thinking, and a willingness to ask whether the old pattern still applies.
点评： 很好的“历史有用，但不能把历史当命运”式回答，量化感很强。

Q14. Should algorithms be allowed to make trading decisions autonomously?

考察点： 技术效率与监管边界的平衡。
回答结构： 有条件允许 + 风险控制 + 人类监督。
I think algorithms can make trading decisions autonomously in clearly defined settings, but only within a strong framework of controls. Algorithms are faster, more consistent, and less emotional than humans in many short-horizon decisions. However, they can also amplify errors, overreact to abnormal signals, or create unintended market effects if poorly designed. So autonomy should never mean absence of accountability. There should be position limits, monitoring systems, emergency stop mechanisms, and human oversight. In finance, efficiency matters, but controllability matters just as much.
点评： 最加分的是“autonomy 不等于 absence of accountability”。

Q15. How should financial institutions use customer data ethically?

考察点： 数据伦理与金融信任。
回答结构： 合法、透明、必要、可解释。
Financial institutions handle highly sensitive data, so ethical use should start with transparency and necessity. Data collection should be tied to a legitimate purpose rather than gathering everything possible simply because it is technically easy. Customers should understand what data is being used and why. I also think explainability matters. If data-driven systems affect approval, pricing, or risk classification, institutions should be able to justify the logic at an appropriate level. In finance, trust is a core asset. Once customers feel monitored without respect, that trust becomes very difficult to rebuild.
点评： 很适合这类专业面试：既讲技术，也讲金融行业最核心的 trust。

分组四：沟通、反思与成长潜力

Q16. Tell us about a difficult problem you could not solve at first.

考察点： 解决问题的韧性与方法。
回答结构： 卡住在哪里 + 如何拆解 + 学到什么。
In one mathematics competition training session, I spent a long time attacking a probability problem with the wrong method because I assumed a direct counting approach would be faster. After getting nowhere, I stepped back and realized the structure was clearer if I reframed it conditionally instead of trying to brute-force every case. I still did not solve it immediately, but changing the framework made the path visible. That experience taught me that getting stuck is not always a sign of weak ability. Sometimes it means the question is asking for a different lens.
点评： 这类题高分点在于：你展示的是方法升级，而不是单纯坚持。

Q17. How would you explain a technical result to a non-technical client?

考察点： 沟通转译能力。
回答结构： 先讲结论，再讲意义，最后讲限制。
I would begin with the decision implication rather than the technical process. For example, instead of starting with model details, I would first say what the result suggests, what level of confidence we have, and what action it supports. Then I would explain the reasoning in simple language, using comparisons or scenarios instead of formulas. Finally, I would mention the limitations, because non-technical clients also deserve to know what the analysis cannot guarantee. I think strong communication in analytics means translating without oversimplifying.
点评： “translate without oversimplifying” 这句话非常适合高分收尾。

Q18. What would you do if your model conclusion turned out to be wrong?

考察点： 对错误的责任感与复盘能力。
回答结构： 先承认 + 查原因 + 修正流程。
My first step would be to acknowledge the error clearly instead of hiding behind technical language. Then I would examine where the failure came from: bad data, weak assumptions, incorrect implementation, or a changing environment the model did not capture. After that, I would focus on two things: correcting the immediate decision impact and improving the process so the same mistake is less likely to happen again. In quantitative work, being wrong is not unusual. The real test is whether you respond with defensiveness or disciplined learning.
点评： 很成熟。尤其把错误拆成 data、assumption、implementation、environment 四类，很像真正在做分析的人。

Q19. Tell us about a time you worked with people who thought very differently from you.

考察点： 协作与认知弹性。
回答结构： 分歧点 + 你的处理方式 + 团队结果。
In a group project, I naturally focused on structure and evidence, while another teammate preferred fast decisions and intuitive ideas. At first, that difference created tension because I felt the discussion was becoming too vague, while they felt I was slowing everything down. Instead of arguing about style, we agreed to separate brainstorming from evaluation. That simple change helped a lot. It gave creative ideas space first and analytical filtering second. I learned that different thinking styles are not automatically a problem. They become a strength when the process is designed well.
点评： 很适合这个专业：既重分析，也不把直觉型同伴简单视为“低质量”。

Q20. Where do you hope this programme takes you in ten years?

考察点： 长期愿景与成长路径。
回答结构： 先能力定位，再谈行业方向，最后谈个人角色。
In ten years, I hope this programme will have shaped me into someone who can combine quantitative rigor with practical judgment. In the early stage, I want to build strong foundations in mathematics, statistics, and programming, and then apply them to finance and analytical decision-making. Over time, I hope to work in a role where I can evaluate risk, interpret complex information clearly, and contribute to more disciplined decisions in a changing market environment. Long term, I do not only want to be technically strong. I want to be trusted for being thoughtful, precise, and responsible.
点评： 结尾落在 trusted、precise、responsible，很符合量化金融方向的成熟气质。
